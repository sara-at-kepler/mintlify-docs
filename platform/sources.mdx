title: "Citations and sources"
description: "Trace every figure to its source, and check coverage with the sourcing report."
---

Citations are the point of Kepler. Every figure in an answer or workbook links to the exact filing or transcript it came from, so results are auditable through the links rather than taken on trust. This is what makes Kepler suitable for work that has to be right.

## Trace a figure to its source

Open the citation on any figure to see the original source, with the matching value highlighted in the document. A workbook cell traces the same way, down to the line in the filing.

## See every source consulted

Each result lists the sources Kepler used, both the ones cited in the answer and everything else it consulted, so you can see the full basis for the work. Sources include SEC filings, earnings call transcripts, and market data.

## Check the sourcing report

Every result includes a sourcing report: a coverage summary that tells you how many figures are cited and whether every number in the answer is backed by a source. When work has to be right, check it before you rely on an answer. It tells you, at a glance, how much of a result is sourced.

<Tip>
  Keep citations attached when you share or paste a result. The links are how a reader verifies a number, so a figure without its link loses the auditability that makes it trustworthy.
</Tip>
